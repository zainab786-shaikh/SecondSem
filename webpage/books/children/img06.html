A Wrinkle in Time



Author: Madeleine L'Engle
Date of publish: 1 January 1962

ABOUT:
A Wrinkle in Time" is a science fiction and fantasy novel written by Madeleine L'Engle and published in 1962. The book has won numerous awards and has become a beloved classic of children's literature. It explores themes of individuality, conformity, love, and the power of the human spirit to overcome adversity.

The book is aimed at young adult readers and tells the story of a young girl named Meg Murry, who is on a quest to save her father from the clutches of evil forces. Along the way, Meg and her companions encounter strange and fascinating creatures, travel through time and space, and learn important lessons about themselves and the universe around them.

The book is notable for its complex and multi-layered plot, its imaginative world-building, and its themes of nonconformity and individuality. It also features strong female characters, which was unusual for its time and has made it a feminist classic.

SUMMARY:

"A Wrinkle in Time" is a science fiction novel written by Madeleine L'Engle and published in 1962. The book tells the story of a young girl named Meg Murry and her quest to rescue her father from the clutches of evil forces.

The story begins with Meg, a thirteen-year-old girl who is struggling in school and dealing with the disappearance of her father, a scientist who has been missing for several years. Meg's mother and her three brothers, Charles Wallace, Sandy, and Dennys, are all worried about her and try to support her in their own ways. However, Meg feels like she doesn't fit in and is plagued by feelings of inadequacy.

One stormy night, Meg and Charles Wallace are visited by a strange old woman named Mrs. Whatsit, who reveals that there is a way to travel through time and space using a concept called a tesseract. She tells them that Meg's father is being held captive on a planet called Camazotz by a being known as the Black Thing. Meg, Charles Wallace, and their friend Calvin O'Keefe embark on a dangerous journey to rescue Mr. Murry and save the universe from the Black Thing's evil influence.

The three children are guided on their journey by Mrs. Whatsit, Mrs. Who, and Mrs. Which, who are revealed to be celestial beings with extraordinary powers. Along the way, they encounter various challenges and obstacles, including a planet where everyone is forced to conform to a strict set of rules, a dark forest inhabited by evil creatures, and a planet where the inhabitants are disembodied brains controlled by a powerful brain known as IT.

As the children face these challenges, they learn important lessons about love, sacrifice, and the power of the individual. They also learn that the universe is a vast and mysterious place, and that there are forces at work that are beyond human comprehension.

In the end, Meg and her friends are able to rescue Meg's father and defeat the Black Thing. Meg learns that her own faults and imperfections are what make her unique and special, and that she has the power to change the world in her own way.

"A Wrinkle in Time" is a classic novel that explores themes of individuality, conformity, and the power of love. It is a timeless story that has captivated readers for generations, and continues to be a beloved work of science fiction and fantasy.
