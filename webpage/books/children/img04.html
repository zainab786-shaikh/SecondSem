The Blue Umbrella



Author: Ruskin Bond
Date of publish: 1980

ABOUT:
"The Blue Umbrella" is a novella written by Ruskin Bond, a well-known Indian author. It tells the story of a young girl named Binya who lives in a small village in the Himalayas and trades her prized leopard-claw necklace for a beautiful blue umbrella. However, her newfound possession leads to a misunderstanding with a shopkeeper and teaches her an important lesson about honesty, friendship, and the value of possessions. The novella is a heartwarming and beautifully written story that captures the charm and simplicity of rural life in India.

SUMMARY:

"The Blue Umbrella" is a novella by Ruskin Bond, set in a small village in the Indian Himalayas. The story revolves around a young girl named Binya, who lives with her family in a small hut near the forest. One day, while herding her family's goats on the hills, she comes across a group of tourists who have come to the village. One of the tourists, a lady, gives Binya a leopard-claw necklace in exchange for some wild raspberries. Binya is thrilled with the necklace, as it is a rare and precious item in her village.

Binya's joy at owning the necklace is short-lived, however, as it soon attracts the attention of the other children in the village, who begin to tease and bully her. Desperate to get rid of the necklace, Binya decides to trade it for something else. One day, she comes across a blue umbrella that belongs to a shopkeeper in the village, Ram Bharosa. Binya is captivated by the beauty of the umbrella and is determined to have it. She persuades Ram Bharosa to trade the umbrella for her necklace.

Binya is overjoyed with her new possession, and the blue umbrella becomes the talk of the village. Everyone admires it and wants to borrow it, but Binya guards it fiercely, not wanting to part with it. However, her possessiveness over the umbrella leads to a misunderstanding with Ram Bharosa, who accuses her of stealing it. Binya is heartbroken and ashamed, and her reputation in the village is ruined.

However, the truth eventually comes out, and Binya is exonerated. Ram Bharosa forgives her, and the two become good friends. Binya learns an important lesson about the value of honesty and friendship, and the story ends on a hopeful note.

"The Blue Umbrella" is a beautifully written story that captures the beauty of the Himalayan landscape and the simplicity of village life. The characters are well-drawn and believable, and the themes of honesty, friendship, and the importance of possessions are explored in a thoughtful and engaging way. The novella is a must-read for anyone who loves a good story and is sure to leave a lasting impression.
