The BFG



Author: Roald Dahl
Date of publish: 1982

ABOUT:
"The BFG" is a children's book by Roald Dahl about a young orphan girl named Sophie who is kidnapped by a giant but soon realizes that he is a kind and gentle creature called the BFG. Together, they go on a mission to stop the other evil giants from harming humans. The book is a heartwarming story about friendship, bravery, and standing up for what is right.

SUMMARY:
"The BFG" is a beloved children's book written by Roald Dahl and published in 1982. The story follows a young orphan girl named Sophie, who is snatched from her bed by a giant in the middle of the night. However, she quickly realizes that her captor, the BFG (short for "Big Friendly Giant"), is not like the other giants who roam the countryside and terrorize humans. Instead, the BFG is kind-hearted and spends his time catching dreams and blowing them into the bedrooms of sleeping children.

Sophie is at first frightened of the BFG, but soon realizes that he is not like the other giants. The BFG tells Sophie about his life and how he is bullied by the other giants because he refuses to eat humans like they do. The other giants are cruel and selfish, and they spend their days snacking on "human beans" and causing chaos. The BFG, on the other hand, is gentle and wise, and he takes Sophie under his wing.

Together, Sophie and the BFG hatch a plan to stop the other giants from causing harm. They decide to visit the Queen of England and ask for her help. With the BFG's knowledge of dreams and his ability to transport people with his magical powers, they travel to Buckingham Palace and meet the Queen.

Initially skeptical, the Queen is eventually convinced by Sophie and the BFG's story and agrees to help them. With the help of the Royal Air Force, they devise a plan to trap the other giants and prevent them from harming anyone else. The BFG uses his powers to catch the other giants and transport them to a far-off island where they can't harm anyone.

In the end, Sophie is hailed as a hero for her bravery in helping to save the world from the other giants. The BFG is also celebrated for his kindness and his willingness to do what's right, even in the face of adversity. The book ends with Sophie and the BFG returning to their home and living happily ever after.

Overall, "The BFG" is a heartwarming story about the power of friendship, bravery, and standing up for what is right. Roald Dahl's signature wit and humor are on full display in this classic children's tale that continues to captivate readers of all ages.
