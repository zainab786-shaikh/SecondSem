The Wonder That Was India



Author: A.L. Basham
Date of publish: 30 May 1998

ABOUT:"The Wonder That Was India" is a book written by A.L. Basham that provides a comprehensive overview of the history and culture of India. The book covers a wide range of topics, including religio n, philosophy, literature, art, science, and politics, and spans the period from ancient times to the arrival of the British in India. .

SUMMARY: The book begins by examining the Indus Valley Civilization, one of the earliest known human civilizations, and goes on to explore the development of Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism. It also covers the rise and fall of empires such as the Mauryas, the Guptas, and the Mughals, as well as the influence of Islam on Indian culture. Basham delves into Indian philosophy, including the schools of Samkhya, Yoga, and Vedanta, and explores Indian literature, including the Vedas, the Ramayana, and the Mahabharata. He also examines the contributions of Indian mathematicians and scientists, including the invention of the zero and the development of Ayurveda, the traditional Indian system of medicine. Throughout the book, Basham provides a nuanced and balanced view of Indian history and culture, highlighting both its strengths and weaknesses. "The Wonder That Was India" is widely regarded as a seminal work on Indian history ad remains an important reference for anyone interested in the country's rich and complex cultural heritage.
