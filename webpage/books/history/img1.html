The Last Mughal



Author: William Dalrymple
 Date of publish: 25 MAY 2006

ABOUT:The book is based on a wide range of primary sources, including Bahadur Shah Zafar's own writings and letters, as well as official records from the British East India Company. Dalrymple also draws on the accounts of contemporary British and Indian eyewitnesses to the events.

SUMMARY:"The Last Mughal: The Fall of a Dynasty, Delhi, 1857" is a historical non-fiction book by William Dalrymple that tells the story of the Indian Rebellion of 1857 and the fall of the Mughal Empire in India. The book focuses on the life of the last Mughal Emperor, Bahadur Shah Zafar, and his role in the rebellion. Dalrymple draws on a wide range of primary sources to provide a detailed and vivid account of life in Delhi before, during, and after the rebellion. He explores the complex relationships between the Mughal court, the British colonial authorities, and the Indian people, as well as the cultural and religious tensions that led to the rebellion. "The Last Mughal" is a well-researched and engaging account of a pivotal moment in Indian history, shedding new light on the complex interactions between India and Britain during the colonial period, and the lasting impact of the events of 1857 on Indian society and politics.
