The daughter of the Sun



Author: Ira Mukhoty
 Date of publish: 30 May 1998

ABOUT: "The Daughter of the Sun" is a compelling and well-written book that brings to life the remarkable story of Rani Lakshmi Bai. It is a must- read for anyone interested in Indian history and the struggle for independence from colonial rule.

SUMMARY: The Daughter of the Sun" is a historical novel based on the life of Rani Lakshmi Bai, the queen of the princely state of Jhansi in North India. The book tells the story of Rani Lakshmi Bai, who led her army against the British during the Indian Rebellion of 1857. The novel explores Rani Lakshmi Bai's childhood, marriage, and rise to leadership. It also delves into the political and social conditions of India during the 19th century, including the British colonization and its impact on Indian society. The book is a well-researched historical fiction that is based on primary sources. It brings to life the remarkable story of Rani Lakshmi Bai and her struggle for Indian independence from British colonial rule. The novel is a must-read for anyone interested in Indian history and the fight for freedom from colonialism.
