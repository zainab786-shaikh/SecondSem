SUMMARY: "Royals and Rebels" by Priya Atwal is a book that explores the complex relationships between the British royal family and the Indian aristocracy during the British Raj. The book uses extensive archival research and interviews to provide a nuanced understanding of the power dynamics at play during this period of colonial history. It delves into the lives of Indian princes and princesses who interacted with the British royals, examining how these encounters impacted both sides. Through the stories of individuals such as Maharaja Duleep Singh and his relationship with Queen Victoria, the book sheds light on the ways in which these relationships were shaped by politics, culture, and personal desires. Overall, "Royals and Rebels" provides a fascinating look into a little-explored aspect of British colonial history and offers new insights into the complex relationships between colonizer and colonized.
