SUMMARY:"Indian Summer" is a historical book by William Dean Howells, first published in 1886. It tells the story of Theodore Colville, a middle-aged American businessman who travels to Florence, Italy, where he reunites with his former love interest, Lina Bowen. The novel explores themes such as the search for happiness and fulfillment, the complexities of love and relationships, and the clash between American and European cultures. Howells' writing style is known for its realism and attention to detail, and the book is considered a classic of American literature. The title "Indian summer" is likely a metaphor for the unexpected warmth and passion that Theodore and Lina experience during their reunion in Italy.
