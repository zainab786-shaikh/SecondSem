SUMMARY:"Invasion: From the Armada to Hitler, 1588-1945" by Kenneth Macksey is a historical work that analyzes military invasions from the late sixteenth century to the end of World War II. The book covers a wide range of invasions and examines each one in detail, providing insights into military s trategies and tactics, political, social, and cultural contexts of the time, and the impact of these invasions on history. The book emphasizes the importance of careful planning and preparation for successful invasions and highlights the challenges faced by invading armies. It also explores the lessons learned from these invasions and their impact on the course of history. Overall, "Invasion" is an informative and engaging work that provides a fascinating perspective on military history and the nature of warfare.
