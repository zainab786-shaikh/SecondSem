ABOUT:
"The Woman in Cabin 10" is a novel by Ruth Ware, published in 2016. It is a mystery and psychological thriller that follows a travel journalist named Lo Blacklock as she sets off on a luxury cruise ship for a week-long assignment. However, one night she wakes up to the sound of a scream and witnesses a woman being thrown overboard from the cabin next to hers, which is supposedly empty.

"The Woman in Cabin 10" was a popular and commercially successful book, and it became a New York Times and Sunday Times bestseller. It received positive reviews from critics and readers alike, and it has been translated into several languages. The book's success helped establish Ruth Ware as a prominent voice in the mystery and thriller genres, and she has since gone on to write several more successful novels, including "In a Dark, Dark Wood" and "The Turn of the Key".

SUMMARY:
"The Woman in Cabin 10" is a novel that tells the story of Lo Blacklock, a travel journalist who is sent on a luxury cruise ship for a week-long assignment. The trip is supposed to be a chance for her to relax and enjoy the amenities of the ship while also writing about her experience for her magazine.

However, on the first night of the voyage, Lo is awakened by a scream and sees a woman being thrown overboard from the cabin next door. She reports the incident to the ship's security, but when they investigate the cabin, they find it empty, and no one on board believes Lo's story.

As Lo becomes increasingly paranoid and anxious, she begins to investigate the mystery on her own. She discovers that the woman in the cabin next door was named Carrie, and she uncovers clues that suggest that Carrie was not who she claimed to be. Lo also meets various suspicious characters on the ship, including Richard Bullmer, the ship's owner, and his wife, Anne, who seem to be hiding something.

As the story progresses, Lo's mental state deteriorates, and she becomes more and more convinced that she is in danger. She receives threatening messages and starts to question her own sanity, struggling to distinguish between reality and hallucination.

One of the most compelling aspects of "The Woman in Cabin 10" is the way that the story is told. The book is written in the first person, from Lo's perspective, which allows the reader to experience her paranoia and anxiety firsthand. The story also features a number of twists and turns that keep the reader guessing until the very end.

The characters in the book are also well-drawn and complex. Lo is a flawed and relatable protagonist, struggling with anxiety and self-doubt, but determined to get to the truth. Richard Bullmer and his wife Anne are mysterious and intriguing, and the other passengers on the ship all have their own secrets and motivations.

As the story progresses, Lo's investigation leads her to a dramatic confrontation with the person who she believes is responsible for Carrie's disappearance. The truth is finally revealed, and Lo discovers the shocking reality of what happened on the ship.

Overall, "The Woman in Cabin 10" is a compelling and suspenseful novel that explores themes of identity, truth, and the human psyche. It is a thrilling page-turner that is sure to entertain anyone who enjoys a good mystery.
