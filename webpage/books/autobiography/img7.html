Wings of Fire



Author: APJ Abdul Kalam
Date of publish: 30 June 2016

ABOUT:
"Wings of Fire" is an autobiography written by A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India. The book provides a detailed account of Kalam's life, from his childhood in Rameswaram to his work as a scientist and later as a public servant. The book begins with Kalam's early years and his fascination with science and technology. He shares his experiences growing up in a small town and his struggles with poverty, while also describing the supportive environment created by his family and teachers. As the book progresses, Kalam recounts his work as a scientist at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), where he made significant contributions to India's missile and satellite programs. He also discusses his leadership roles in various scientific organizations and his work in promoting education and innovation in India.

SUMMARY:
Throughout the book, Kalam emphasizes the importance of perseverance, hard work, and dedication to achieving one's goals. He also reflects on his spiritual beliefs and how they have influenced his life and career. Overall, "Wings of Fire" is an inspiring and insightful autobiography that provides a glimpse into the life of one of India's most respected public figures. It is a testament to Kalam's vision, leadership, and commitment to using science and technology for the betterment of society, and a valuable source of inspiration for readers of all ages.
