The Story of my Life



Author: Helen Keller
Date of publish: 30 June 2016

ABOUT:
"The Story of My Life" is an autobiography written by Helen Keller, a deaf-blind American author, activist, and lecturer who overcame her disabilities to become an important figure in the disability rights movement. The book begins with Keller's account of her early years, including her experience of being struck with an illness that left her deaf and blind at 19 months old. Despite her disabilities, Keller learned to communicate with the help of her teacher, Anne Sullivan, who taught her to use sign language and Braille. As Keller grew older, she became a prominent figure in the disability rights movement, advocating for equal rights and opportunities for people with disabilities. The book provides a unique insight into Keller's personal journey, including her struggles to communicate and her eventual acceptance into Radcliffe College, where she graduated with honors.

SUMMARY:
Throughout the book, Keller emphasizes the importance of education and perseverance, as well as the need for society to recognize the potential of people with disabilities. She also shares her experiences of traveling the world and meeting with political leaders and social activists. Overall, "The Story of My Life" is a powerful and inspiring autobiography that highlights the resilience and determination of one of the most important figures in the disability rights movement. It is a must-read for anyone interested in disability rights, education, and the power of the human spirit to overcome adversity.
