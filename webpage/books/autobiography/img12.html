Abraham Lincoln



Author: Lord Charnwood
Date of publish: 30 June 2016

ABOUT:
"Abraham Lincoln" by Lord Charnwood is a well-regarded biography of the 16th President of the United States. The book offers a detailed account of Lincoln's life, from his childhood in Kentucky and Indiana, to his rise in politics in Illinois and his eventual election as President. Charnwood delves into Lincoln's personal and political philosophy, as well as his accomplishments and challenges as President during the tumultuous period of the Civil War. One of the key themes of the book is Lincoln's commitment to preserving the Union and ending slavery, which Charnwood describes as a deeply held conviction rather than a political calculation. Charnwood also discusses the complex relationships Lincoln had with his Cabinet members, military leaders, and fellow politicians, including his sometimes contentious interactions with generals such as George McClellan and his complicated friendship with Secretary of State William Seward.

SUMMARY:
Throughout the book, Charnwood provides insights into Lincoln's character and personality, describing him as a thoughtful and compassionate leader who was able to inspire those around him during a time of great national crisis. He also explores Lincoln's enduring legacy as a symbol of American democracy and freedom. Overall, "Abraham Lincoln" by Lord Charnwood is a well-written and comprehensive biography that offers a thorough and insightful examination of one of America's most important and iconic historical figures.
