Born Again on the Mountain



Author: Arunima Sinha
Date of publish: 30 June 2016

ABOUT:
The book you may be referring to is "Born Again on the Mountain: A Story of Losing Everything and Finding It Back" by Arunima Sinha. Arunima Sinha is the first female amputee to climb Mount Everest. In her book, she shares her personal story of overcoming incredible challenges and setbacks, including losing a leg in a tragic train accident, to achieve her dream of climbing the world's highest peak. The book begins with Sinha's childhood in rural India, where she faced discrimination and prejudice as a young girl. Despite these obstacles, she was determined to succeed and became a national-level volleyball player. However, in 2011, Sinha's life changed dramatically when she was thrown off a moving train by robbers. She lost her left leg in the accident and faced a long and difficult recovery. But rather than giving up, Sinha was inspired to set a new goal for herself: to climb Mount Everest.

SUMMARY:
"Born Again on the Mountain" chronicles Sinha's journey from tragedy to triumph. It details the physical and emotional challenges she faced during her training and her eventual climb to the summit of Everest in 2013. The book also explores Sinha's personal philosophy of perseverance and determination, which she believes can help anyone overcome even the most daunting obstacles. Overall, "Born Again on the Mountain" is a moving and inspiring memoir that showcases the power of human resilience and the importance of pursuing one's dreams in the face of adversity.
