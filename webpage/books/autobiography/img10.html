My Inventions



Author: Nikola Tesla
Date of publish: 30 June 2016

ABOUT:
"My Inventions" is an autobiography of Nikola Tesla, a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, and futurist who is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electrical system. In the book, Tesla shares his personal experiences, including his childhood in Serbia and his education in Prague and Vienna, where he developed his interest in electricity and magnetism. He then describes his move to the United States and his work with Thomas Edison, where he helped to develop direct current (DC) electrical systems, before eventually leaving Edison's company to pursue his own projects.

SUMMARY:
Throughout the book, Tesla shares his many inventions and discoveries, including the Tesla coil, the AC motor, and wireless communication. He also discusses his struggles to gain recognition for his work, as well as his personal beliefs and philosophy. "My Inventions" provides readers with a unique insight into Tesla's thought process and creative approach to problem-solving. It is an inspiring and enlightening read for anyone interested in the history of technology and the life of one of the most important inventors of the modern era.
