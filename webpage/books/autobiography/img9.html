The Story of My Experiments with Truth



Author: Mahatma Gandhi
Date of publish: 30 June 2016

ABOUT:
The autobiography of Mahatma Gandhi, also known as "The Story of My Experiments with Truth," is a memoir written by Gandhi himself, detailing his life from childhood through his struggles as a political and social activist in India's fight for independence. Gandhi writes about his early years, including his upbringing in a devout Hindu family and his experience studying in London, where he was exposed to Western ideas and society. He then goes on to describe his political and social activism, including his involvement in campaigns against discrimination, poverty, and colonial rule in India. Throughout the book, Gandhi reflects on his personal beliefs, including his commitment to non-violent resistance as a means of achieving political and social change. He also discusses his experiments with vegetarianism, celibacy, and other forms of self-discipline and spirituality.

SUMMARY:
The book offers a rare glimpse into Gandhi's personal life, including his relationship with his family and friends, as well as his struggles with his own flaws and limitations. Gandhi also shares his views on a range of social and political issues, including caste, religion, and nationalism. Overall, "The Story of My Experiments with Truth" is a powerful and insightful autobiography that provides a unique perspective on one of the most important figures in modern history. It is a must-read for anyone interested in Indian history, social activism, and the power of personal beliefs and convictions in effecting change.
