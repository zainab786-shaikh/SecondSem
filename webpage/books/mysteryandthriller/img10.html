The Hunger



Author: Alma Katsu
Date of publish: 6 March 2018

ABOUT:
"The Hunger" is a novel by Alma Katsu that was published in 2018. It is a historical,mysterious fiction novel that reimagines the tragic story of the Donner Party, a group of pioneers who became stranded in the Sierra Nevada mountains during the winter of 1846-1847 and were forced to resort to cannibalism to survive.

In Katsu's novel, she adds a supernatural twist to the story, introducing a mysterious and malevolent force that haunts the members of the Donner Party throughout their journey. The novel is told from multiple perspectives, including those of the party's leaders, their family members, and a few fictional characters.

Overall, "The Hunger" is a gripping and atmospheric horror novel that combines historical fiction with supernatural elements. Katsu's reimagining of the Donner Party's story is both chilling and thought-provoking, and she does an excellent job of building tension and suspense throughout the book.

SUMMARY:
"The Hunger" by Alma Katsu is a historical fiction novel that tells the story of the ill-fated Donner Party, a group of pioneers who attempted to cross the Sierra Nevada Mountains in 1846. The novel adds a supernatural twist to the real-life events, and explores themes of fear, desperation, and the lengths to which people will go to survive.

The novel is narrated from multiple perspectives, including those of several members of the Donner Party, as well as a few fictional characters. One of the central characters is Tamsen Donner, the wife of George Donner, who is portrayed as a strong and intelligent woman struggling to keep her family and the other members of the party alive.

As the party progresses through the mountains, they begin to experience a series of strange and unsettling events. Some members of the party become ill, while others are plagued by nightmares and visions of a mysterious and malevolent force that seems to be stalking them through the wilderness. Food and supplies become scarce, and tensions rise as members of the party begin to turn on one another.

As conditions worsen, the group is forced to make a series of difficult decisions in order to survive. They are forced to resort to cannibalism, consuming the flesh of their deceased companions in order to stave off starvation. The novel explores the psychological toll that this decision takes on the members of the party, as well as the long-term impact of their experiences on those who survive.

Katsu does an excellent job of building tension and suspense throughout the novel. She explores the inner lives of the characters, delving into their fears and motivations, and creates a palpable sense of unease that permeates the entire book. The supernatural elements of the story add an extra layer of horror to the already-gruesome tale, creating a deeply unsettling atmosphere that is sure to stay with readers long after they finish the book.

Overall, "The Hunger" is a gripping and atmospheric novel that is both chilling and thought-provoking. Katsu's reimagining of the Donner Party's story is masterfully done, and she does an excellent job of exploring the psychological and emotional toll that the real-life events must have taken on those who experienced them. The novel is a powerful reminder of the lengths to which people will go in order to survive, and the devastating consequences that can result.
