My Sister The Serial Killer



Author: Oyinkan Braithwaite
Date of publish: 20 November 2018

ABOUT:
My Sister, the Serial Killer is a 2018 thriller novel by Nigerian writer Oyinkan Braithwaite. Braithwaite's debut novel, it was originally published in Nigeria as an e-book entitled Thicker Than Water in 2017, before being released in the United States by Doubleday Books on November 20, 2018.

Korede has long been in love with a kind, handsome doctor at the hospital where she works. She dreams of the day when he will realize that she's exactly what he needs. But when he asks Korede for Ayoola's phone number, she must reckon with what her sister has become and how far she's willing to go to protect her.

SUMMARY:
"My Sister, the Serial Killer" is a fascinating and thought-provoking novel that explores complex issues such as loyalty, family, and the consequences of one's actions. The book, written by Oyinkan Braithwaite and published in 2018, takes place in Lagos, Nigeria, and follows the story of two sisters, Korede and Ayoola.

The novel opens with Korede receiving a call from her sister Ayoola, who has just killed her third boyfriend in self-defense. Korede, who is a practical and responsible nurse, rushes to the scene to help her sister dispose of the body and cover up the crime. This has become a familiar routine for Korede, who has helped her sister cover up her murders in the past, but this time, things are different. Ayoola sets her sights on Tade, a doctor at the hospital where Korede works and the man Korede secretly loves. Korede is forced to make a difficult decision: should she continue to cover up her sister's crimes or risk everything to protect the man she loves?

One of the strengths of "My Sister, the Serial Killer" is the unique perspective from which the story is told. The novel is narrated by Korede, who is a compelling and complex character. As the older sister, Korede feels responsible for Ayoola, even though she is troubled by her sister's behavior. Throughout the book, we see Korede struggle with her loyalty to her sister and her growing sense of guilt and responsibility for the crimes they have committed. Korede's voice is strong and distinctive, and the reader is immediately drawn into her world and her struggles.

In addition to exploring the relationship between the sisters, "My Sister, the Serial Killer" also touches on larger societal issues. The novel sheds light on the treatment of women in Nigeria, particularly the prevalence of violence against them. Through the lens of the sisters' relationship, Braithwaite highlights the ways in which women are often pitted against each other and the struggles they face in a patriarchal society. This adds an extra layer of depth and complexity to the novel, making it not only an entertaining read but also a thought-provoking one.

Despite the dark subject matter, "My Sister, the Serial Killer" is infused with humor and wit, making for an engaging and entertaining read. Braithwaite's writing is sharp and clever, and she has a talent for balancing serious subject matter with moments of levity. The novel is a quick read, but it leaves a lasting impression on the reader, thanks in part to its memorable characters and its unique perspective on the nature of family and loyalty.

In conclusion, "My Sister, the Serial Killer" is a captivating and thought-provoking novel that combines dark humor, social commentary, and a compelling narrative to create a unique and memorable reading experience. Through the story of two sisters, the novel explores complex issues such as family dynamics, the treatment of women, and the consequences of one's actions. It is a book that will keep you engaged from beginning to end, and one that will stay with you long after you've finished reading.
