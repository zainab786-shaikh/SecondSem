The Magic of the Lost Temple



Author: Sudha Murthy
Date of publish: 11 November 2015

ABOUT:
"The Magic of the Lost Temple" is a children's novel written by Indian author Sudha Murthy. It was published in 2014 and is targeted towards readers aged 10 and above. The book is a work of fiction, but it incorporates elements of history and culture to educate young readers about India's rich heritage

"The Magic of the Lost Temple" is a captivating and inspiring read for young readers, as it combines adventure, mystery, and valuable life lessons in a way that is both educational and entertaining. Sudha Murthy's vivid descriptions of the Indian countryside and its temples bring the story to life and make it a must-read for anyone interested in India's rich cultural heritage.

SUMMARY:

"The Magic of the Lost Temple" by Sudha Murthy is a delightful story about a young girl named Nooni, who lives in Bangalore with her parents. Nooni is fascinated by her grandmother's stories about the temples of India and the history behind them. Her curiosity leads her to embark on a journey of adventure and discovery that takes her to the heart of India's rich cultural heritage.

One summer, Nooni's parents decide to send her to her grandparents' village in Karnataka to spend her holidays. Nooni is initially reluctant to leave the comforts of the city, but she soon finds herself captivated by the beauty of the village and its surroundings. She discovers an old, abandoned temple in the forest behind her grandparents' house, which piques her interest.

Nooni is intrigued by the temple and decides to explore it. She discovers a mysterious book with no title in the temple that has been lost for many years. Nooni is determined to find out the story behind the book and the lost temple. She meets a boy named Raju, who lives in the village and helps her in her quest. Together, they uncover the history of the temple and the book, which turns out to be a valuable manuscript that has been missing for centuries.

As they delve deeper into the mystery of the book and the temple, they realize that they are not the only ones interested in the treasure. A group of treasure hunters is also searching for the manuscript, and Nooni and Raju must face many challenges along the way to keep it safe. They must use their wit, intelligence, and bravery to outsmart the treasure hunters and uncover the secrets of the lost temple.

Throughout the story, Nooni learns about the importance of preserving India's rich cultural heritage and the need to protect it from those who would exploit it for their own gain. She discovers the beauty of India's ancient temples and the stories they hold, and realizes that it is her responsibility to preserve them for future generations.

"The Magic of the Lost Temple" is a captivating tale that takes readers on a journey of discovery and adventure, while also imparting valuable lessons about history, culture, and the importance of preserving our heritage for future generations. Sudha Murthy's vivid descriptions of the Indian countryside and its ancient temples bring the story to life, making it a wonderful read for people of all ages. The book also highlights the value of friendship, courage, and determination in the face of adversity, making it an inspiring read for young readers.
