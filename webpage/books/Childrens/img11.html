Hansel and Gretel



Author: Jacob Ludwig Carl Grimm, Wilhelm Grimm
Date of publish: 20 December 1812

ABOUT:
"Hansel and Gretel" is a classic fairy tale originating from Germany. The story follows two siblings who get lost in the woods and stumble upon a gingerbread house owned by a witch who tries to eat them. The siblings use their wits to outsmart the witch and return safely to their father. The story teaches children about resourcefulness, bravery, and caution when dealing with strangers.

SUMMARY:

"Hansel and Gretel" is a popular fairy tale that originated in Germany. The story is believed to have been first recorded by the Brothers Grimm and has been retold in various forms throughout the years.

The story follows two siblings, Hansel and Gretel, who are abandoned in the woods by their parents during a famine. As they wander through the forest, they come across a gingerbread house, which they start to eat as they are very hungry. However, the house belongs to a witch who lures them inside with the promise of more food.

The witch then captures Hansel and forces Gretel to do chores for her. However, Gretel is clever and manages to outsmart the witch by shoving her into the oven and rescuing her brother. The two siblings then find their way home and are joyfully reunited with their father, who had been searching for them since their mother had died.

The story has been adapted into various forms of media, including picture books, operas, and films. It is often used to teach children about the importance of being resourceful, brave, and careful when dealing with strangers. The fairy tale also highlights the themes of hunger, poverty, and survival in difficult times.

The story of "Hansel and Gretel" has been interpreted in various ways by different cultures and artists. Some versions of the story focus more on the horror elements, such as the cannibalistic witch, while others emphasize the themes of family, love, and bravery. The story has become a beloved classic and continues to inspire new adaptations and retellings to this day.
