Rapunzel



Author: Brothers Grimm
Date of publish: 1997

ABOUT:
"Rapunzel" is a classic fairy tale that originated in Germany. The story follows a young girl who is locked in a tower by a wicked witch as punishment for her parents stealing vegetables from the witch's garden. The tale has been adapted into various forms of media, including picture books, movies, and TV shows.

The story has endured through the years due to its engaging plot and relatable themes. It teaches children about the consequences of breaking promises, the importance of patience and perseverance, and the power of love. It also highlights the dangers of trusting strangers and the potential for jealousy and betrayal.

Overall, "Rapunzel" remains a beloved fairy tale that continues to capture the imaginations of children and adults alike. Its timeless themes and captivating story have ensured that it will remain a classic for generations to come.

SUMMARY:

"Rapunzel" is a classic fairy tale that originated in Germany. The story follows a young girl who is locked in a tower by a wicked witch as punishment for her parents stealing vegetables from the witch's garden. The witch tells Rapunzel that she will only be allowed to leave the tower if she promises to never leave, but the girl eventually falls in love with a prince who climbs up her long hair to visit her.

The witch regularly visits Rapunzel in the tower and uses her long hair to climb up to the top. The witch uses the visits to threaten and scare Rapunzel, telling her that she will harm the prince if she ever tries to escape or tells him about the tower. Despite this, Rapunzel and the prince plan to escape, and the prince brings a length of silk to use as a rope to climb up the tower.

One day, the witch discovers the prince visiting Rapunzel and cuts off her long hair, causing the prince to fall from the tower. The prince is blinded by thorns when he lands, but he wanders around the forest and eventually hears Rapunzel singing a lullaby. Rapunzel is overjoyed to see the prince and cries, which restores his sight.

The two lovers plan their escape again, but the witch discovers their plans and tries to stop them. In the end, the witch falls to her death, and Rapunzel and the prince are finally able to leave the tower and live happily ever after.

The tale has been adapted in various forms of media, including picture books, movies, and TV shows. It is often used to teach children about the consequences of breaking promises, the importance of patience and perseverance, and the power of love. The story also highlights the themes of jealousy, betrayal, and the dangers of trusting strangers.
