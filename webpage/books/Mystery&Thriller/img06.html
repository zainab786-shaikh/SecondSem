The Whisper Man



Author: Alex North
Date of publish: 13 June 2019

ABOUT:
"The Whisper Man" is a psychological thriller novel by Alex North. It was published in 2019 and quickly became a bestseller, receiving critical acclaim for its intricate plot and suspenseful storytelling. The novel has been compared to the works of Stephen King and Gillian Flynn, and has been praised for its atmospheric setting and well-drawn characters. The novel has also been optioned for a film adaptation. Overall, "The Whisper Man" is a compelling and haunting read that is sure to leave a lasting impression on readers.

SUMMARY:
"The Whisper Man" is a haunting and complex novel that deals with themes of grief, trauma, and the nature of evil. The book is narrated from multiple perspectives, including those of Tom Kennedy, his young son Jake, and DI Amanda Beck, the lead investigator on the case.

Tom is a writer who has recently lost his wife in a car accident. He decides to move to the small town of Featherbank with his young son Jake in an attempt to start a new life. However, the town has a dark history. Twenty years earlier, a notorious serial killer known as the Whisper Man abducted and killed several young boys in the area. The killer was eventually caught and imprisoned, but the murders had left a lasting mark on the town.

Shortly after Tom and Jake move into their new home, a young boy named Neil Spencer goes missing. As the police begin to investigate, they realize that the circumstances of Neil's disappearance bear striking similarities to the Whisper Man's crimes. As the investigation progresses, it becomes clear that the killer may have returned to Featherbank, and Tom and Jake find themselves caught up in the dark secrets of the town.

Tom is struggling to connect with his son, who claims to have been talking to an imaginary friend named "the boy in the floor." Tom is haunted by his own difficult relationship with his father, and the loss of his wife has only intensified his feelings of guilt and grief. Meanwhile, DI Beck is dealing with her own traumatic past and the pressures of solving a high-profile case.

The tension in the novel builds steadily as the investigation deepens. The characters must navigate a web of lies, secrets, and half-truths as they try to uncover the truth about the Whisper Man's identity. As they delve deeper into the case, they discover that the killer may have had an accomplice, and that the truth about the murders may be more shocking than anyone could have imagined.

"The Whisper Man" is a masterfully written novel that explores the complexities of human relationships and the dark side of human nature. The book has been praised for its atmospheric prose, intricate plotting, and gripping suspense. It is a must-read for fans of psychological thrillers and true crime, and is sure to keep readers on the edge of their seats until the very end.
